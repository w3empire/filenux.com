McAfee Stinger is a standalone antivirus program created by McAfee, Inc. It is designed to detect and remove specific viruses and other malware types from a computer. It is typically used to remove known malware infections when a full antivirus scan is unable to detect or remove the malware.

Overview

When it comes to keeping your computer safe from viruses and other malicious software, a full-featured anti-virus program is a must. But even the best anti-virus software can sometimes miss certain threats, which is where McAfee Stinger comes in.

McAfee Stinger is a standalone utility that is specifically designed to detect and remove certain types of viruses and malware that may have slipped past your main anti-virus program. It’s not meant to replace your primary anti-virus software, but rather to serve as a specialized tool to assist administrators and users when dealing with an infected system.

McAfee Stinger comes with next-generation scan technology, which includes rootkit scanning and scan performance optimizations. This allows it to quickly and efficiently identify and remove threats that are included on the “Threat List” option under the Advanced menu options in the Stinger application.

Along with its powerful scanning capabilities, McAfee Stinger also now includes Raptor, a real-time behavior detection technology that monitors suspicious activity on an endpoint. Raptor leverages machine learning and automated behavioral-based classification in the cloud to detect zero-day malware in real-time. This means that even if a new type of malware has not yet been identified and added to the Threat List, Raptor may still be able to detect and remove it.

It bears mentioning that McAfee Stinger is not a replacement for a full anti-virus program but rather an additional layer of protection to help keep your computer safe. It’s also important to keep the software up to date, as details on new or enhanced signatures added with each Stinger build are listed in the Readme details.
